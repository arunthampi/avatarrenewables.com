Horse Powered Generator

Horse drawn bus services, launched by George Shillibeer during early 19th century, was a popular mode of transport for Londoners, which later evolved into horse drawn trams. A basic twin-horse drawn bus or tram used to carry 20-25 people at an average speed of 10 kms / h (1400 kgs x 166.66 m/min = 38.136 kW). Aim of this project is to revive this ‘vintage’ raw horse power to electrical energy, as zero carbon energy.

The concept is based on an unmanned horse cart generator, running through fixed metal tracks. The cart wheel will drive a permanent magnet generator (PM-Generator), through a gear box or chain drive (of suitable ratio). The PM Generator output through power electronics and voltage step-up device, will deliver the voltage to overhead lines and rail (similar to trams). Horses will be controlled by Rein/Bridle and Whip controller, with wireless remote control and monitoring feature.

Multiple HPGs running on a large circular rail, with power collection overhead line can easily feed sufficient electric power to local electrical distribution network or operate stand-alone electric trams in small townships.
